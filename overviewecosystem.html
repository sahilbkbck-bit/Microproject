üåä Pollution & Plastic

Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste enter our oceans, threatening marine life and ecosystems worldwide.

Plastic Crisis

Over 8 million tons of plastic enter the ocean annually. This pollution forms massive garbage patches and breaks down into microplastics that infiltrate every level of the marine food chain.

Marine Life Impact

Sea turtles, whales, and seabirds mistake plastic for food. Entanglement in fishing gear and plastic debris kills hundreds of thousands of marine animals each year.

Chemical Pollution

Industrial waste, oil spills, and agricultural runoff introduce toxic chemicals into ocean waters, poisoning marine ecosystems and contaminating seafood.

üåçClimate Change

Rising temperatures and ocean acidification are fundamentally altering marine ecosystems.

Ocean Warming

Oceans absorb 90% of excess heat from global warming. Rising temperatures cause coral bleaching, disrupt migration patterns, and force species to relocate to cooler waters.

Acidification

Increased CO2 absorption makes oceans more acidic, threatening shellfish, coral reefs, and organisms with calcium-based shells and skeletons.

Sea Level Rise

Melting ice caps and thermal expansion raise sea levels, flooding coastal habitats and threatening human communities living near the shore.

üêü Overfishing

Unsustainable fishing practices are depleting fish populations faster than they can recover.

Depleted Stocks

Over 30% of global fish stocks are overfished, with many commercially important species on the brink of collapse. Industrial fishing operations harvest fish faster than populations can reproduce.

Bycatch Problem

Unintended catch of dolphins, turtles, and non-target fish species results in massive waste. For every pound of target fish, up to five pounds of other species are caught and discarded.

Ecosystem Imbalance

Removing key species disrupts the ocean food web, causing cascading effects throughout marine ecosystems and threatening biodiversity.

üå≥ Habitat Destruction

Critical marine habitats are being destroyed at alarming rates, eliminating nurseries and homes for countless species.

Coral Reef Loss

50% of coral reefs have been lost in the past 30 years due to warming waters, pollution, and destructive fishing. Reefs support 25% of all marine life despite covering less than 1% of the ocean.

Mangrove Destruction

Coastal development destroys mangrove forests that serve as vital nurseries for fish and protect coastlines from storms. Over 35% of mangroves have been cleared globally.

Seabed Damage

Bottom trawling scrapes the ocean floor, destroying ancient deep-sea corals and habitats. This practice turns thriving ecosystems into underwater wastelands.
